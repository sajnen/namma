ABOUT US

We are Nisa and Meenakshi, two undergraduate students driven by the belief: our city should work for everyone who lives in it. Our journey began as part of the Young Leaders for Active Citizenship (YLAC) Mobility Championship, where we launched the Namma Nildana campaign. In close collaboration with the Association of People with Disabilities (APD), we are working to audit, advocate for, and transform the bus stops along Sarjapur Road. Our goal is to ensure these spaces are not only infrastructurally sound but universally accessible, ensuring that age or disability never dictates a person's freedom to move. We believe that a better city starts with better access, and Namma Nildana is our first step toward making that a reality.

Project Overview

Namma Nildana project audited around 40 bus stops in Sarjapur road between Sarjapur Police station and Iblur junction. The audit look at the infrastructural quality of bus stops and if they are accessible to people with disabilities. The audit also looks at safety features within bus stops. The map section of this website has an interactive map that gives details regarding each and every bus stop we audited.

Purpose

In a Metropolitan city like Bengaluru public transport is the most convenient and economically friendly option of transport. However the current conditions, lack of infrastructure, and lack of maintinance makes using the current Bus transport system in Bengaluru. The purpose of this project is to document bus stop conditions, in order to ask for better conditions that we as citizens deserve.
